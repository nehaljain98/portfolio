The Story
Spotify is a popular audio streaming and media services provider. The app offers a lot of different social features that distinguishes itself from other competitors and one of them is a collaborative playlist. The feature provides the ability to let your friends add their own tracks to your playlist. The collaborators can add or delete tracks at any time by dragging them into the playlist or pressing the delete button to remove them from the playlist; all the changes are visible on everyone‚Äôs copy of the playlist.
However, this feature is rarely used and most friends are not collaborating on a playlist. Users are not putting in the time to create playlists together.
The objective of this feature is to create an easy experience for users to stream everyone's choice of songs on one device in group settings.
Problem Space
Why does Spotify need this feature?
Every time my friends and I are together, the music listening experience is mostly terrible. People argue over song sleections and whose music will be queued next. Making a collaborative playlist takes a lot of steps, and some users have no idea what they can do with the "make collaborative" button. Since you need more effort and time, users tend to be stressed about the social implications of the added songs. Moreover, this feature will help people test the waters before using collaborative playlists which stay in your library.
Space for opportunity
What if we focused on allowing customers to collaborate short-term?
Since creating a collaborative playlist takes time and effort, I decided to create a short-term feature that would take the pressure out of this situation for the users. Moreover, users will be able to assemble songs catered towards a specific vibe that they are going for at that moment. This would improve every group hangout and event.
The Solution
The Assemble feature would allow users to create a group in which every member could add songs to and play from the same queue. Besides providing users with an easy collaborative music streaming experience, it would also help Spotify increase its social currency because more people would want to use spotify to be able to play their songs in a group setting.
How did we get there?
User Research
Concept Testing
For the first step of my research, I chose 12 random users to participate in concept testing. For this test, I focused on discovering and making playlists & social features. I invited the 12 users to do three tasks:
1.Discover a playlist of dance songs
2.Create a new playlist called 'Dance Dance Music' and add a few songs from the playlist you discovered
3.Invite a friend to collaborate on your playlist 'Dance Dance Music'
Result- 8 out of 12 users failed to invite friends to collaborate on the playlist.
User Pain Points
The following are the core problems this design aims to solve:
Adding and deleting songs in the assemble feature - Users need to be able to add and remove songs from a playlis when they are in close proximity to each other. This requires a bluetooth enabled device that will allow to detect the group queue.
Adding and Removing MembersThe admin should be able to add and remove members for the assembled group. The admin should also be able to give admin access to members.
View Names - Just like in the collaborative playlist feature, the user should be able to view the songs and the names of users who added the song.
Editing the Line UpThe users should be able to change the line up by dragging songs up/down to change their queue number.
What were the next steps?
Ideation
User Flow
Organizing and grouping user flows for effecient navigation.
I decided to compare and contrast the current user flow of collaborative playlist with the user flow of the Assemble feature.
I showed the paper prototype to the 12 participants and asked them to go through the entire process of going to the assemble button, creating an assemble or joining a group, adding songs to the group queue and managing the group (add/remove members & exit the group) on paper. 9 out of 12 participants were able to finish the task without asking any questions. 3 users had trouble giving admin access to the members. I further modified the admin access by allowing the admin to clicking on the user's name and switch between member and admin.

What did I learn?
Reflection
With a delightful collaborative music streaming experience, Spotify would stand out from its competitors and continue increasing brand loyalty and attracting more users due to social currency. In addition, partnering with social media apps (Messenger, Instagram, etc.) on using Assemble would also give users more incentives and ways to use Spotify Premium. However, implementing a new feature requires a lot more testing and iterating than what I had considered in this case study. I would be interested in dwelling deeper into the research and usability testing that would allow us to understand truer results of this feature.
