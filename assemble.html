A Group Spotify Feature

June - July 2020

Spotfiy Assemble is a feature that will allow users in close proximity to form a group and play songs together by queuing music.

I decided to do this study to gain understanding of user flows and how user research impacts the designs.

Context

Overview

Spotify is a popular audio streaming and media services provider. The app offers a lot of different social features that distinguishes itself from other competitors and one of them is a collaborative playlist. The feature provides the ability to let your friends add their own tracks to your playlist. Collaborators can add or delete tracks at any time by dragging them into the playlist or pressing the delete button to remove them from the playlist; all the changes are visible on everyone’s copy of the playlist.
However, this feature is rarely used and most friends are not collaborating on a playlist. Users are not putting in the time to create playlists together.
The objective of this feature is to create an easy experience for users to stream everyone's choice of songs on one device in group settings.

Need

Every time my friends and I are together, the music listening experience is mostly terrible. People argue over song selections and whose music will be queued next. Making a collaborative playlist takes a lot of steps, and some users have no idea what they can do with the "make collaborative" button. Since you need more effort and time, users tend to be stressed about the social implications of the added songs. Moreover, this feature will help people test the waters before using collaborative playlists which stay in your library.

Timeline

Over the eight week timeline, I dwelved into user research. I spent three weeks conducting surveys and interviews which left me with roughly 4-5 days to create a prototype.

Project Brief 

Since creating a collaborative playlist takes time and effort, I decided to create a short-term feature that would take the pressure out of this situation for the users. Moreover, users will be able to assemble songs catered towards a specific vibe that they are going for at that moment. This would improve every group hangout and event.

The Assemble feature would allow users to create a group in which every member could add songs to and play from the same queue. Besides providing users with an easy collaborative music streaming experience, it would also help Spotify increase its social currency because more people would want to use Spotify to be able to play their songs in a group setting.

⚡️ The Challenge: Conduct user research to create an original spotify feature

Current Design

Why is a collaborative playlist not meeting this need?

  • Long Lasting - It is a permanent playlist that stays in your library
  • Social Anxiety - People become conscious of what they are listening when other people have access to the playlist as well

There is an opportunity here for Spotify to use its social currency for a collaborative music listening experience instead of only collaborative playlists

Current User Flow for Collaborative Playlist

User Research

Concept Testing

For the first step of my research, I chose 12 random users to participate in concept testing. For this test, I focused on discovering and making playlists & social features. I invited the 12 users to do three tasks:
1. Discover a playlist of dance songs
2. Create a new playlist called 'Dance Dance Music' and add a few songs from the playlist you discovered
3. Invite a friend to collaborate on your playlist 'Dance Dance Music'
Result- 8 out of the 12 users failed to invite friends to collaborate on the playlist.

User Pain Points

The following are the core problems this design aims to solve:

Adding and deleting songs in the assemble feature - Users need to be able to add and remove songs from a playlist when they are in close proximity to each other. This requires a bluetooth enabled device that will allow to detect the group queue.
Adding and Removing Members - The admin should be able to add and remove members for the assembled group. The admin should also be able to give admin access to members.
View Names - Just like in the collaborative playlist feature, the user should be able to view the songs and the names of users who added the song.
Editing the Line Up - The users should be able to change the line up by dragging songs up/down to change their queue number.

User Flow

Organizing and grouping user flows for effecient navigation.
I decided to compare and contrast the current user flow of collaborative playlist with the user flow of the Assemble feature.
I showed the paper prototype to the 12 participants and asked them to go through the entire process of going to the assemble button, creating an assemble or joining a group, adding songs to the group queue and managing the group (add/remove members & exit the group) on paper. 9 out of the 12 participants were able to finish the task without asking any questions. 3 users had trouble giving admin access to the members. I further modified the admin access by allowing the admin to click on the user's name and switch between member and admin.

Validation

Final design feature:
• Go to "Assemble" via Your Library
• Create an assembly by adding users or sharing a bar code
• Add songs to the queue from your playlists or by searching on Spotify
• Join a group by accepting an invitation or scanning a bar code
• Manage group: adding/removing members, switching admin access, editing the line up
• End the assemble: End the queue by exiting & removing the queue

Reflection

With a delightful collaborative music streaming experience, Spotify would stand out from its competitors and continue increasing brand loyalty and attracting more users due to social currency. In addition, partnering with social media apps (Messenger, Instagram, etc.) on using Assemble would also give users more incentives and ways to use Spotify Premium. However, implementing a new feature requires a lot more testing and iterating than what I had considered in this case study. I would be interested in dwelling deeper into the research and usability testing that would allow us to understand truer results of this feature.
