The Story
The UC Davis CStem website is one of the most visited university websites. The site has a lot of different users, but is especially relevant to the 30,000+ K-12 student population that use the site at some point in their robotic and coding career as it contains all the information about the program requirements. Unfortunately, as you will see in the "Personas" section of this case study, users find the site unappealing and are often unable to find the information that they need. As a result, both students and faculty are frustrated with the poor web experience.

The objective:

My student team was tasked with gathering data about the site‚Äôs users, and came up with mockups to explore what a redesigned site might look like. Our objective was to redesign a multi-purpose website that would achieve the center's goal and serve user efficiently.

My Role:

I designed and prototyped the branding and layout of the website. Valeria and I contributed to user research, interviews and surveys. We worked frequently and closely with the project manager who was supervising our work.
Problem Space
Why is the current website design not working?
The old site‚Äôs bad user experience has been a source of frustration for UC Davis students and faculty, so university administrators asked us to explore solutions to the site's problems. We conducted user research and used UC Davis style guides to inform our redesign, and validated our work using a final prototype, which we would present to a group of university stakeholders..
Current Experience
Why aren't users engaging with the online presence of this service?
The current website deisgn lacks a good information architecture and overwhelms users with loads of information. In this quick demo, we can see that finding information is difficult because the navigation bar has no clear value path. Moreover, due to consistent updates, there are duplicate pages which would confuse users even more.

The Solution
We designed a set of new features within the JAPA app that supports a much wider range of needs. The new design takes new and returning users on a more controlled, personalized journey and uses unique features such as the map quick look to list view, filtering, and garage level card swipe to guide drivers smoothly to their destination. By integrating these features and mimicking the in-app journey to the external parking experience, JAPA creates a more full, guided experience for users and builds a more lasting relationship with them.
How did we get there?
User Research
Surveys
We chose to use a survey to discover what the different user segments are. We deliberated on whether an online or an in-person survey was more appropriate. We picked the most effecient user reseach method given our limited resources and decided on online survey.
We posted the survey on various faculty and student groups on Facebook. We also emailed school administrators with the survey. In total, we got 56 responses from students and 26 from administrators. Through the survey we gained an understanding of who was using the site, and what they were using it for.

Strategy
Search and getting to information are a priority. The majority of users enjoy looking for specifics instead of browsing through all the information. We also wanted to take inspiration from the Davis Housing magazine and use methods from editorial design so that the listings base is a delight to read.

To further narrow the search, users can customize their preferences with the filter feature.

Ideation
Information Architecture
Since the organization of information on the website was an issue brought up in our personas, we wanted to take a look at the persona use cases and see:

1. Where their final page destinations were on the sitemap
2. What user navigation paths looked
Wireframes
After inserting, removing, and rearranging pages on the sitemap the team was able to start sketching new website layouts. As we sketched and wireframed, we explored layouts that followed existing common patterns, but also explored new features to give the design an edge.During the wireframing process, we decided that it was important to focus on the social features that allow sharing and reacting. We also incorporated a modern redesign which would reduce their cognitive load. Abiding by stylistic standards was significant to gaining the traction and audience that we wanted.
