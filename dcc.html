Design Careers Club

President 2019-2020

Design Careers Club is a registered student organization that hosts student workshops, alumni events and design careers fair for over 3000 students and alumni.

As the President and the Creative Director, I guided the design team in the visual interface and user experience design to develop the Design Careers Club website. I lead the conceptualization of our brand identity and designed the logos and illustrations. 

Context

Overview

DCC is a student-run organisation that supports and encourages its participants to explore design related professional opportunities by hosting events and workshops. The team prepares all year for multiple opportunities so that students can explore design interests with the help of alumni and industry leaders.

My Role

As the President of the Design Careers Club, I designed the website with a talented team over the course of a quarter. I created wireframes, addressed and considered user profiles, and implemented the best course of action for the club.

Timeline

We recruit new team members in the Spring quarter and begin event preparation and design development over the summer break. Therefore, we used spring quarter as an estimated timeline to design the website so that it was ready for the new leadership.

User Research

Interviews

To get more understanding of what these usersâ€™ goals and pain points on the site were, we conducted more in-depth interviews where we would walk through the website with 5 people from each user group. We asked them both preestablished and followup questions about their goals and habits as users. Based of those interviews, we were able to create personas (who are fictional people with motivations and oppinions that average those of the people interviewed).

Users and Personas

Who are our users? Since the user types are straight forward, we categorized them into 5 user groups:

Design Students: This website will be used by the students to navigate design workshops, design events and job opportunities. They can also refer to the blog which will have professional articles such as preparing for the events.
Design Faculty: The design faculty will have a similar goal of being aware of the events. They will also need to be able to get information on alumni and events from the previous years.
UC Davis Design Alumni: The alumni will be connected to current design students through this website and will initially begin their mentorship program throught the website.
Recruiters: The recruiters will be encouraged to connect and employ current design students that they meet at club events.
The DCC Board: This website will need to be accessed and edited by the board members. They will maintain relevant information which requires editing to be easy and the site to work for longevity.

User Pain Points

The following are the core problems this design aims to solve:
1. Publicize job opportunities and professional workshops
2. Connect design students and alumni through the mentorship program
3. Promote the club's events and newsletter

Ideation

Information Architecture

Organizing and grouping user flows for effecient navigation.
Since the organization of information on the website was an issue brought up in our personas, we wanted to take a look at the persona use cases and see:
1. Where their final page destinations were on the sitemap
2. What user navigation paths looked
3. The person icons indicate the ending page for each of the personas' navigation paths.

We wanted to make sure that all of our personas, especially the students, could access their information quickly without having to read a lot of text. So we restructured the site to facilitate the access of information from the use cases, (the information that they desired most).

Wireframing

We wanted to connect the site's information architecture to its visual design by showing paths between pages. Wireframing aided us in clarifying consistent ways for displaying particular types of information on the user interface. As you can see from the sketches, we explored intended functionality in the interface and worked on different landing pages. Our main focus was on the home page because there isn't a lot of information so we wanted to gain and keep attention for the users to be able to reach the destination they wanted.

Validation

Prototype

At this point in the process, we're increasing fidelity, moving from wireframes to mockups. After finalizing the ideas generated in the wireframes, we built our prototype. This prototype contains the pages relevant to each of the use cases since we would use it for subsequent usability tests.

Reflection

In the end, the succession of leadership is responsible for the continuation of the website. We took on the responsibility of creating the website based on user research. We still feel that the organisation should do a lot more research into design systems because there was an overwhelming number of synonymous design patterns for club organisations. Moreover, we would have preferred to code the website as we had the skills to do so but unfortunately we decided to go with Weebly so that it is easy to edit for the future board members. We think that the website will benefit from being coded and hosted as the interaction and animation will improve the website. However, it is important to understand the limits and parameters of a project and we respected the criterion.
